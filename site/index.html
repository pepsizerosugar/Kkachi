Why Kkachi

A magpie for things that should come back.

Kkachi means 까치, the Korean magpie.

In Korean memory, the magpie is tied to good news, cleverness, and return. That is the shape of the product: it quietly moves what is idle, then makes return feel obvious.

You are not leaving every tab open because it is useful. You are leaving it open because deciding is mildly annoying, and because it might be useful later.

Most of those pages will not be revisited. Kkachi accepts that human pattern instead of pretending another tab manager will fix it.

What Kkachi does

It tucks away the tabs that have stopped earning space.

Watch

Read only the tab address and title.

Kkachi uses macOS Apple Events and avoids page content, form state, cookies, and scripts.

Prune

Close only tabs that pass the policy.

Active, protected, ambiguous, or unavailable tabs are skipped instead of guessed.

Restore

Keep the path back short.

The menu keeps a capped local history for recent pruned tabs and routes restore to the browser.

Privacy model

The promise is deliberately narrow.

Kkachi is built around a smaller claim that can be checked in source: it stores only the minimum restorable context and keeps it on your Mac.

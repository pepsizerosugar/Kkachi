Why Kkachi

Why a magpie?

Kkachi means 까치.

In Korea, magpies are tied to good news, cleverness, and coming back. This app borrows that idea: clear what is idle, keep it easy to bring back.

Most tabs are not work. They are postponed decisions.

You might read it later. You probably will not. Kkachi handles that gap without making you manage tabs again.

What it does

Watch. Close. Restore.

Watch

Reads tab title and URL.

Kkachi uses macOS Apple Events and avoids page content, form state, cookies, and scripts.

Prune

Closes stale tabs.

Active, protected, ambiguous, or unavailable tabs are skipped instead of guessed.

Restore

Keeps recent tabs local.

The menu keeps a capped local history for recent pruned tabs and routes restore to the browser.

Privacy

Only what restore needs.

Kkachi stores URL, title, browser, and time. It does not read page content or send tab data anywhere.
